What is black hole gravity?

Black hole gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape. This extreme pull is caused by an enormous amount of mass being compressed into an incredibly small space, which distorts spacetime to an extreme degree. While they have immense gravitational pull, objects can orbit black holes from a safe distance, and the gravitational force is the same as any other object of the same mass at that distance, as explained by NASA and AstroCamp.
